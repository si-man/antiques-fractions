A Bustling Marketplace

In recent years, many consumers have started turning their backs on the mass-produced ‘sameness’ of the past couple of decades, looking to surround themselves with finely-crafted items with character which might even have stories to tell. The advent of online auctions has also made antiques more accessible to a wider range of people.

This upturn in antique dealing is evidenced by the vast number of antiques fairs taking place; in the UK alone there are well over 300 scheduled for 2019. Indeed, the figures from regional auction houses are very positive:

Why Collect Antiques?

Portfolio Diversification: With zero correlation to stock markets, alternative assets provide a valuable diversification tool in times of economic uncertainty, such as the Brexit era.

Asset-Backed: Real assets like antiques always carry residual value, unlike typical stock portfolios.

Hedging Against Inflation: High-grade antiques have commonly demonstrated +200-300% price evolution since the turn of the century.

Ownership Satisfaction: Unlike traditional assets, antiques provide us with aesthetic pleasure and carry cultural significance. You can take enjoyment from owning them.

Antique Fractions

Antiques for All

Antique Fractions offers a unique opportunity; we are pioneering a concept whereby people from all walks of life can start their own antique collection, with a wide variety of items to choose from.

An antique fraction is a percentage of an antique. Each item is broken down into a certain number of fractions, depending on its value, to get a standard £50 starting value for each fraction. The more valuable the piece, the more fractions are available.

Each item in our collection is listed on the website with a countdown clock which usually starts at 18 or 36 months until the predicted date of sale. However, should a private buyer make an acceptable offer prior to this date, we will offload the item earlier. In the event of a lot not selling at auction, it will be entered into auctions every month thereafter. When it is sold, you receive back the current value of your fraction(s); if the item has appreciated by 30% then your fractions will also have appreciated by that amount.
