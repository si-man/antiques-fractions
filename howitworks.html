HOW IT WORKS

Antique Fractions offers a unique opportunity; we are pioneering a concept whereby people from all walks of life can start their own antique collection, with a wide variety of items to choose from.

An antique fraction is a percentage of an antique. Each item is broken down into a certain number of fractions, depending on its value, to get a standard £50 starting value for each fraction. The more valuable the piece, the more fractions are available.

Firstly please sign up for a membership. This will give you access to our full range of antiques and collectibles to choose from. If fractions of a particular item you like are still available, simply click on buy and complete the purchase with PayPal or credit/debit card. We do not charge a commission or any management fees for maintaining the stock. We do take 20% of the profit from any sale of fractions whether they are traded on our platform or we sell the item at auction.

Frequently Asked Questions:

What are my exit and redistribution options?

When the item is sold, you will have a choice to either receive the current value of the fraction back or use it to purchase your next antique.

Can I withdraw my fraction?

Yes, you can withdraw the value of your fraction once the item has been sold*. (Please check the countdown clock on the item carefully before purchasing fractions, so you can be sure that the predicted sale date fits your desired timeline.)

Why buy fractions?

A lot of antiques are priced beyond an individual’s reach, and even if you share the cost with friends, all eggs are placed in a single basket. With art fractions, you can embark upon an affordable personal journey collecting pieces which appeal to your own taste.

The appreciation as a percentage is the same on a single fraction as it would be on the whole antique but at an affordable price.

It makes a unique gift to purchase for family or friends.

In excess of 20% per year is possible on a modest outlay of funds.

(*We strongly recommend against early withdrawal of fractions. However, if you really want to do so, it would be impractical to physically remove your section of the item. Therefore, you would be treated as a private buyer and required to pay twice the value of the entire antique.)
